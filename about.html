In each and every country around the world that had wars and even organizations, all prioritize on remembering their fallen heroes with memorial days and stones sculpted in them their names. These memorials are done to pay tribute to the bravery and sacrifices of soldiers. They ensure that the names and stories of those who fought and died are not forgotten. Memorials help preserve history by providing the future generations with a tangible connection to the past and remind them of the events and people that shaped their nation today. The memorials of the fallen martyrs will eternally help educate and teach what the martyrs fought for, what true and rightful causes they stood with against evil and inhuman. They are the compass of the people in the roughest seas during their darkest hour into salvation. The number of people martyred and casualties in any war could reach a hundred-thousand or millions, this poses a great issue in preserving the data of people that died. In addition, having a stone slab written on it the birth, death date of the person with his name is not enough today in our digital world and especially that each and every kid growing up today for a dozen generations to come would need an actual and realistic hero that one stood ground on this earth with his righteous cause having his own story and special fingerprint on society. Their true right of these fallen martyrs is to be remembered by society, and their righteous right is to actually care for their families, provide for them anything they need because they have taken a share in the sacrifice of a fellow member in their family. The main solution to this issue is to create a website linked to a server that hosts the data of the martyrs. The data will mainly focus around their stories, good deeds, where they grew up, where and how did they die and for what cause they were fighting for. This website will include martyrs during different wars of our beloved Jabal Aamel, each enlisted in the specified war.
